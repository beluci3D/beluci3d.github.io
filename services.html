ESP32 Project Enclosure
PROBLEM
Customer built a custom ESP32 sensor node but had no way to mount it cleanly. Exposed PCB, loose wires, no protection.
SOLUTION
Designed a snap-fit enclosure in Fusion 360 with cable management, ventilation slots and a magnetic lid for easy access.

Exactly what I needed — fitted perfectly on the first print. The magnetic lid is a genius touch, makes changing the firmware so easy. Will definitely order again.

// PROJECT_002 · Mechanical · PETG
Discontinued Machine Part
PROBLEM
A small bracket on a workshop machine snapped. Part was discontinued — no spare available anywhere. Machine was unusable.
SOLUTION
Measured the original broken part, modelled an improved version in Inventor with added reinforcement ribs. Printed in PETG for heat resistance.

I thought the machine was done for. Beluci measured the broken part from my photos and had a replacement to me in 3 days. It's been running ever since — stronger than the original.

// PROJECT_003 · Desk Setup · PLA
Custom Desk Cable System
PROBLEM
Customer had a non-standard desk frame with no cable management solution. Generic clips didn't fit the unusual rail profile.
SOLUTION
Designed a parametric clip system in Fusion 360 that snaps onto the exact desk profile. Printed a full set of 12 clips + cable tray in matte black PLA.

Sent over some measurements and photos and got a perfect fit back. The clips look like they came with the desk. Setup looks completely clean now — totally worth it.

// PROJECT_004 · Product Design · PLA + PETG
Product Prototype Housing
PROBLEM
Small startup needed functional prototypes of their smart home device housing to test form factor and show to investors. Injection moulding too expensive at this stage.
SOLUTION
Modelled the housing in Creo based on their technical brief, printed 3 iterations with refinements. Final version was presentation-ready with smooth surface finish.
